Usability Testing Results

  • Images and Visuals

    Speaking to my user afterwards, I learned that the timeline could use better visuals. In her words, "it would have been nice to see an image of each invention". For increased interactivity, I think it would be good to add a button or hover attribute so that the user can pull up an image of each invention if they desire or not. In the 'Forbes' article, it would also help break up the text by adding images or graphs.

  • Navigation Buttons

    My usability tester found the site navigation to be somewhat clunky. After the test, she reported a confusion with the coloring and effect of the back/forward arrows. Particularly, because they weren't grayed out it seemed as if there was something to go to before she had even nagivated the site. Also, at the bottom of the timeline it was inconvienent to have to scroll all the way back to the top of the page after reading.

  • Starting Point

    After clicking through pages with information and stories, the Clock page was severely overwhelming. My user said she would have liked to see it come first, in order to set the scene. Possibly, this Clock page could be the Home page that users begin on. And on it, there would be buttons to the other pages and maybe a description of the project.
